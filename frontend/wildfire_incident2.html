Eaton Fire
Los Angeles, Near Altadena Drive and Midwick Drive, Altadena/Pasadena
Description:

The Eaton Fire ignited on January 7, 2025, in Eaton Canyon, located in the San Gabriel Mountains near
Altadena, California. Fueled by powerful Santa Ana winds, the wildfire rapidly spread, consuming over
14,000 acres and destroying more than 9,400 structures. Tragically, the fire resulted in 17 fatalities
and displaced over 100,000 residents. The blaze persisted for 24 days before being fully contained on
January 31, 2025.

Investigations into the cause of the Eaton Fire have focused on the potential involvement of electrical
equipment operated by Southern California Edison (SCE). Eyewitness accounts and video evidence suggest
that faulty power lines near the origin of the fire may have sparked the initial flames. In response,
numerous lawsuits have been filed against SCE, alleging negligence in maintaining their infrastructure
and failing to de-energize lines during critical fire weather conditions. The company is currently conducting
internal investigations to determine the exact cause of the fire.
