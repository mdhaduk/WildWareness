Community Shelters at Capacity as Wildfire Spreads

Source: Emergency Management Office
Date: February 13, 2025
Category: Community Impact
Region: Central California
Reporter: Sarah Martinez

Local emergency shelters are reaching maximum capacity as more residents evacuate from areas threatened by the expanding wildfire.

Emergency management officials report that several community shelters in Central California are operating at or near capacity. Additional facilities are being prepared to accommodate the growing number of evacuees.

Current Shelter Status

Most primary shelters are now at 90% capacity or higher. Emergency services are working to open secondary locations and coordinate with neighboring counties for additional support.

Community Response

Local organizations and volunteers have mobilized to provide essential supplies and support to affected residents. Donations of food, water, and basic necessities are being accepted at designated collection points.

Important Information:

  • Shelter Information Hotline: 1-800-555-7890
  • Donation Coordination: 1-800-555-3456
  • Volunteer Registration: 1-800-555-8901
